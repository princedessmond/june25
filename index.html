⚠️ Safety Tips When Encountering Police

1. Stay Calm

Remain peaceful and composed. Do not provoke or engage in arguments.

2. Record Everything

If safe, record police interactions on video. This provides evidence if needed.

3. Know Your Rights

You have the right to peaceful assembly (Article 37). Police must respect this.

4. Stay in Groups

There's safety in numbers. Don't separate from fellow protesters.

5. Legal Aid Ready

Call our 24/7 hotline immediately: 0800 587 423

6. Move to Safety

If tear gas is deployed, move upwind and to a safe area. Rinse eyes with water.

About the Protest

Why We March

On June 25th, Kenyans from all walks of life will gather peacefully in Nairobi CBD to exercise our constitutional right to peaceful assembly and make our voices heard on critical national issues affecting our nation.

Our Demands for a Better Kenya

1. Economic Justice & Youth Employment

  • Create 1 million jobs for young Kenyans
  • Lower cost of living - reduce prices of essential goods
  • Stop excessive taxation on small businesses
  • Transparent use of public funds
  • Support for startups and entrepreneurship

2. Good Governance & Anti-Corruption

  • Prosecute corrupt government officials
  • Strengthen EACC and independent oversight bodies
  • Publish all government contracts online
  • End wasteful government spending
  • Implement the two-thirds gender rule
  • Reduce size of cabinet and government

3. Quality Education & Healthcare

  • Increase funding for public schools and universities
  • Implement free primary and secondary education fully
  • Improve NHIF/SHA healthcare coverage
  • Hire more teachers and doctors
  • Renovate dilapidated schools and hospitals

4. Police Reforms & Justice

  • End extrajudicial killings and enforced disappearances
  • Police accountability and oversight
  • Justice for victims of police brutality
  • Independent Police Oversight Authority (IPOA) empowerment
  • Community policing initiatives

5. Democracy & Constitutional Rights

  • Protect freedom of assembly and expression
  • Stop harassment of protesters and activists
  • Ensure free and fair elections
  • Respect for judicial independence
  • Full implementation of the 2010 Constitution

6. Environmental & Climate Action

  • Stop deforestation and protect water catchment areas
  • Clean and safe drinking water for all
  • Renewable energy investment
  • Protect Nairobi's green spaces
  • Climate change adaptation programs

Our Vision

We envision a Kenya where every citizen has access to quality education, affordable healthcare, decent employment, and lives in dignity. A Kenya where public resources benefit the people, not a privileged few. A Kenya where the rule of law prevails and constitutional rights are respected. A Kenya where young people have hope and opportunity.

This is not a political movement. This is a people's movement for a better Kenya.

Our Commitment to Peace

This is a strictly peaceful demonstration. We are committed to non-violence and respect for all persons and property. Any acts of violence or destruction do not represent our movement. We will exercise our constitutional rights with dignity and discipline.

Code of Conduct

  • Remain peaceful and non-violent at all times
  • Respect law enforcement officers
  • Do not damage property - public or private
  • Stay together in groups for safety
  • Follow instructions from protest organizers
  • Help fellow protesters in need
  • No alcohol or drugs during the march
  • Respect all Kenyans regardless of political affiliation

Historical Context

This movement builds on Kenya's rich history of peaceful mass action - from the independence struggle to the fight for multiparty democracy in the 1990s, to the successful resistance against oppressive taxation in 2024. When united, Kenyan citizens have the power to demand accountability and drive positive change.
